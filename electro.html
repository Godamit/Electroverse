WHAT IS ELECTROVERSE TSEC ?

Electroverse.Comm-TSEC, the technical committee of the Electronics and Telecommunication (EXTC) Department at Thadomal Shahani Engineering College (TSEC), Mumbai, has been at the forefront of fostering innovation and collaboration since its inception in 2003. The committee’s mission is to promote interdisciplinary cooperation and hands-on learning experiences, empowering students to apply theoretical knowledge to real-world challenges.Over the years, Electroverse.Comm has organized a variety of technical events, workshops, and competitions that encourage creativity and teamwork. One of its flagship events is the Non-Stop Hardware Hackathon, a unique platform where students from different departments—such as Computer Science, IT, and Electronics—come together to collaborate on hardware and software solutions. This cross-disciplinary environment enriches the participants' problem-solving approaches, allowing them to leverage diverse perspectives and skills.

DEPARTMENT OF ELECTRONICS AND TELECOMMUNICATION

The Department was established in the year 2002 with a clear view of being a forerunner in the field of Telecommunications. The Department seeks to evolve towards excellence and adapt itself to the rapid advancements in specialized communication-related fields. This Department earlier had the nomenclature of Electronics Engineering from 1983 to 2001 with a student intake of 120. From 2002, the student intake is 60.

The department was awarded accreditation by the National Board of Accreditation during 2008–2011, and reaccreditation from 2016 to 2019. The department has also started a PhD program with an intake of 10 students from the academic year 2014-15.

The program’s curriculum is designed to cater to the ever-changing needs and demands of the communication industry. The focus is on holistic learning and empowering students to make significant contributions at all levels. The open elective system gives students a handful of choices to develop expertise in areas like Neural Networks, Radar Engineering, Microelectronics, Data Compression and Encryption, Satellite Communication, and Image Processing. The department features state-of-the-art hardware and software laboratories with a special focus on telecommunications.

CODING WEEKENDS
10th August, 2024 - Semester End

Coding Weekend, organized by the Electroverse Committee, is an EXTC-inclusive online coding competition held every Saturday. The event is designed to boost students' coding skills and prepare them for placements, aptitude tests, and internship opportunities. Conducted on a dedicated platform, it offers a competitive yet supportive environment where participants can enhance their problem-solving abilities while receiving valuable career guidance. By fostering consistent practice, Coding Weekend helps students build the coding expertise necessary for success in the tech industry. At the end of each semester, the top coders are awarded exciting prizes, with the top five performers’ names displayed on the department’s television, celebrating their achievements and motivating others to excel.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can participate in this Hackathon?

Participants can be a student of any year of the B.E.(Bachelor of Engineering)/ B.Tech Degree college or any Technical Diploma College.

Wrong details are submitted in the registration form, how can they be corrected?

Contact the Student Coordinators (Contact details are mentioned on the next page) or mail to electroversecommtsec@gmail.com

Will Food and wireless internet access be provided?

Yes, there will be. However, it is always better to be prepared: If you have a backup option, please feel free to bring it along and Food, Lunch, Snacks, and Dinner will be provided throughout the event.

What should teams carry to the Hackathon Venue?

Students are requested to bring their own devices and other necessary equipment required for the event. The components required to build the solution are to be informed by the qualified Teams after Round 1, which will be arranged for them by the Thadomal Shahani Engineering College.

What is the benefit of participating?

Self-assess your abilities while coordinating with your team, Get tips from the Experts, enhance social skills, seize the opportunity to win prize money of up to Rs 25,000/- and have fun along with an effective and memorable learning experience.

Is it mandatory to form teams with all members from the same college?

No, you can form your team with all members who qualify the eligibility criteria.
